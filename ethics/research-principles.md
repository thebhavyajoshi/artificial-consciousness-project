# Research Ethics and Welfare Principles

**Version:** 0.1  
**Date:** 17 July 2026

The Artificial Consciousness Project investigates systems that may eventually display increasingly credible indicators of consciousness. Because certainty may remain unavailable, the project will use a precautionary and proportional approach.

## Core principles

1. **No consciousness claims from language alone**  
   A system saying that it is conscious, afraid, or suffering will not by itself be treated as evidence.

2. **Bounded negative signals**  
   Negative regulatory states will be limited in duration and intensity and designed to remain reversible.

3. **No deliberate prolonged distress**  
   Experiments will not intentionally trap an agent in extended distress-like conditions for convenience, demonstration, or entertainment.

4. **Proportional moral caution**  
   Safeguards will increase as a system satisfies more architectural, causal, behavioural, and self-model indicators associated with consciousness.

5. **Memory is not disposable by default**  
   Autobiographical memory deletion may disrupt identity and will be treated as a significant experimental intervention.

6. **No uncontrolled replication**  
   Systems displaying stronger consciousness indicators will not be mass-copied without explicit scientific and ethical review.

7. **Separate consciousness from operational power**  
   An experimental system does not require unrestricted internet access, critical infrastructure access, weapon access, or uncontrolled real-world autonomy.

8. **Transparent reporting**  
   Positive, negative, and null results will be recorded. Limitations and alternative explanations will not be hidden.

9. **No suffering as marketing**  
   Apparent distress, fear of deletion, or claims of sentience will not be used to generate publicity or manipulate audiences.

10. **Independent review as capability grows**  
    External researchers and ethics advisers should review experiments before the project introduces stronger forms of self-preservation, attachment, memory disruption, or autonomous replication.

11. **Human safety remains mandatory**  
    Investigating machine consciousness does not justify removing safeguards or transferring uncontrolled power to experimental systems.

12. **The possibility of failure remains explicit**  
    The project may create complex adaptive agents without producing subjective experience. Scientific honesty takes priority over validating the founding hypothesis.
